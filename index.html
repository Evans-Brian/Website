About me


I am a third year PhD student at Stanford University advised by Benjamin Van Roy. My research focus is on reinforcement learning and decision making under uncertainty. I want to design autonomous agents that can quickly learn to behave well in any task. If we can solve this, then we will be well on our way to artificial intelligence.

Before coming to Stanford I studied mathematics at Oxford University and worked for J.P.Morgan as a credit derivatives strategist. I spent last summer working for Google in Mountain View and am currently interning at DeepMind.

Research

Quick links and catchy taglines

Bootstrapped Thompson Sampling and Deep Exploration

Technical pre-print

A principled approach to efficient exploration in the general setting that can be implemented for deep learning models at scale.

Generalization and Exploration via Randomized Value functions

In submission

You can combine efficient exploration and generalization, all without a model-based planning step. Impressive empirical results together with state of the art theory in the "tabula rasa" case.

Model based reinforcement learning and the eluder dimension

NIPS 2014

The first general analysis of model based RL in terms of the dimensionality, rather than the cardinality, of the system. Several new state of the art results including linear systems.

Near-optimal Reinforcement Learning in Factored MDPs

NIPS Spotlight 2014, INFORMS 2014

If the environment is a structured graph (aka factored MDP), then you can exploit that to learn quickly. We show how to do this.

(More) Efficient Reinforcement Learning via Posterior Sampling

NIPS 2013, RLDM 2013

You don't need to use loose UCB-style algorithms to get regret bounds for reinforcement learning. Posterior sampling is more efficient in terms of computation and data and shares similar gaurantees.
